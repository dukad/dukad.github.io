CIRCUIT PUZZLE TOOL

JUL 2022 - DEC 2022

ENERGY EFFICIENT CIRCUITS AND SYSTEMS GROUP

BOSTON, MA

The Circuit Puzzle Tool is a five-year project, and I was given the opportunity to start it from scratch. The project is meant to create a web-based educational tool focused on electrical engineering concepts to be used alongside curriculum for high-school and college students. During my time on the project, I implemented both a JavaScript, HTML, and CSS front-end as well as a Python backend for the site. I implemented a framework for problems to be generated and prompt answers as well as an online SPICE simulator. To do so, I first implemented the control system for users to place down circuit components on the website. I implemented a depth-first search algorithm to generate the netlist of a circuit, which was then sent to the Python server to complete the math portion of solving circuits. This project helped me develop my algorithms, learn web-development, and solidify my knowledge of circuit theory.

POWERHOUSE MARINE

SEP 2022 - DEC 2022

GENERATE PRODUCT DEVELOPMENT STUDIO

BOSTON, MA

My first Generate project, PowerHouse Marine, is a deployable solar array for marine living. The product was inspired by the fact that many living for long periods at sea carry generators for electricity and do not have the space on their boats for more than a few solar panels. PowerHouse Marine will allow these people to deploy solar panels from the side of their boat while anchored, allowing for a more eco-friendly energy alternative and allowing boats to be at sea for longer periods of time without having to refuel. I designed the deployment mechanism for this product, using a STM-32 microcontroller to interpret inputs and control the deployment of the solar panels. The design featured a PWM controlled motor along with an encoder to track the position of the solar panels. The encoder communicated position back to the microcontroller using a PWM signal, and I utilized interrupts and timer configuration to read this signal react accordingly. Our software automatically slowed and stopped the motor once the solar panels had deployed to their final length. The design included basic button input and a voltage buck to reduce the voltage from the boat’s battery. Our prototype allowed for an additional 1200 Watts of power to be generated per module.

CUSTOMIZABLE TOY ROBOT

JAN 2022 - APR 2022

FORGE

BOSTON, MA

The customizable toy robot was a project designed for FORGE at Northeastern under the prompt “developmental toys”. We wanted to create a product that would allow kids to explore STEM concepts in a fun way and at a young age. I designed the electrical system for the toy, using an Arduino microcontroller to manipulate various motors and the controller. I implemented RF modules on the robot and the controller in order to allow the user to control the robot from a distance. The robot featured detachable servo motors with different parts attached to them to allow for customization. We included DC motors to allow the robot to move, and designed different wheels to allow for further customization.
